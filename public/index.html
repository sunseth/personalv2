Clean Hack | Emissions Visualization

d3 js + angular js + mongodb
At Berkeley's Clean Hackathon I pulled public government data about emissions, threw it into a db, and visualized it.
Put the emissions into mongodb, parsed and transformed lat/long coords to their US county, summed up emissions by tons and by company, then displayed using d3 cloropleth. Darker = more pollution.
Cloropleth is interactive and clicking on a county brings up a pie chart in real time with angular js. We can see emissions by type and the worst offenders in that county in the year 2011.

NFTE | Network for Teaching Entrepreneurship

socket io + passport js + angular js
Parterned with a non profit at PayPal's Opportunity Hack to bring students and mentors together
Students and mentors sign up, input what they're interested in or can teach, then get automatically matched. They can send emails and chat with one another to facilitate online learning.
Click here to try.
Students should select the 'Learning' tab, while Mentors select the 'Teaching' option. You get matched on exact word matches of what you're interested in. To chat, person A opens a chat from his/her 'Profile' page, while person B finds person A on his/her dashboard. Person B opens the chat of person A.


PayPal | Hermes Dashboard

three js + d3 js + odometer js
Visualize paypal checkout transaction volume with mock data. Three js for the flowing cube and d3 js for the shifting bar graphs. Odometer js for the rotating numbers.
Transitions every minute to show data across the week and the last hour. The front end listens for data from the server that streams updates each minute, then the models are repopulated.
